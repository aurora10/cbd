1. How does CBD lotion work?

CBD lotion works by delivering cannabidiol-rich hemp oil or isolated cannabidiol molecules into the layers of your dermis. The skin is the body’s largest organ, so it’s a prime candidate for cannabidiol administration. Plus, with topical administration of CBD, it’s possible to target specific areas and provide localized effects.

Keep in mind that the effects of CBD are often bolstered by terpenes, depending on which type of cannabidiol-rich hemp extract you’re using. Terpenes are fascinating oils that naturally occur in many members of the plant kingdom, and depending on its genetics, a cannabis strain may express any number of different terpenes such as limonene and beta-caryophyllene. Each terpene tastes and smells different, and they can potentially boost the effects of CBD depending on which terpenes are present in the hemp oil and in what quantities.

Broad-spectrum and full-spectrum CBD oil also contain trace cannabinoids other than cannabidiol such as CBG, THCV, and CBDa. In most cases, these other cannabinoids are not present in sufficient quantities to produce any noticeable effects, but around the country, interest in isolating and marketing cannabinoids such as CBG and CBN is increasing rapidly.

Oil-based and water-based CBD topicals work differently, and the effects of CBD lotions and balms are also altered by their delivery mechanisms and ingredients. We’ll touch on some of these finer points as we continue.

These wayward atoms leave oxidative stress in their wake, which can cause inflammation and pain or simply eat away at your cells until they have a hard time reproducing and maintaining themselves. The skin’s aging process isn’t entirely irreversible, however, and it’s possible to take measures to mitigate these changes, as many common skincare products aim to do.
