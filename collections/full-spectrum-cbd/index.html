What Is Full Spectrum CBD?

Full spectrum CBD oil is the oil that is initially extracted from the buds, leaves and stems of the hemp plant. Full spectrum CBD gives you all of the valuable compounds found in hemp, including all of the cannabinoids, terpenes, flavonoids, and some healthy fatty acids. Those cannabinoids include the two major cannabinoids, CBD (cannabidiol) and THC (tetrahydrocannabinol), as well as over a hundred minor cannabinoids, including CBG, CBN, and CBC. Hemp contains only trace amounts of THC (by law, less than 0.3%), which is not enough to be intoxicating.

What Is Delta-9 THC?

While there are different cannabinoids with THC in their names, delta-9 THC is the one we generally associate with the shortened name of THC. Tetrahydrocannabinol is one of the two major cannabinoids in cannabis and is the psychoactive ingredient in marijuana. While it is the cannabinoid that can get you high, delta-9 THC also has a wide range of wellness benefits.

Benefits of Full Spectrum CBD + Delta-9 THC

The benefit of full spectrum CBD is that it allows users to experience the Entourage Effect, which is a natural phenomenon that occurs when multiple cannabinoids and other cannabis compounds are consumed together. When this happens, the various compounds actually make each other work much harder than they would naturally work on their own in the body’s endocannabinoid system. So, for instance, the CBD in a full-spectrum CBD product will actually have a stronger effect than it would if you took it on its own (in, say, a CBD isolate product, which is 99% pure CBD).

The THC content in full spectrum CBD oil is, by law, less than 0.3% of the dry weight of the hemp. But in actuality, that content is usually much less than the legal limit. That’s why we’ve created our Delta-9 THC line of full spectrum products. In these innovative products, we’ve increased the amount of THC to be much closer to the legal limit for hemp-derived CBD products. That means more THC, and more Entourage Effect. Your CBD will work harder. The other cannabinoids, terpenes, and flavonoids will work harder. And your THC will work harder for you!

We won’t make a medical recommendation here (which is illegal), but we will share with you some of the ways that people use THC products. The benefits these people have experienced from the use of THC products include mood elevation (which can help with stress), pain relief, antihistamine effects, digestion (particularly for nausea relief), immune and infection support, antioxidant benefits, muscle relaxant, and sleep support.

  1. Stress

People have used THC for centuries to relax and ease their stress. When THC activates receptors in the brain, it can bring a feeling of elation or mood elevation (there’s a reason that the feeling one gets from consuming massive amounts of THC is called “getting high”). Mitigating stress can potentially have beneficial effects for longer term issues, like anxiety and some of the symptoms of post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD). However, we cannot make any medical claims in regards to the latter. It should also be noted that when taken in large amounts, THC can trigger paranoia and anxiety in some people.

  1. Pain

Many people take THC to help manage pain. This is one of the areas in which medical marijuana has been used, such as with cancer patients. THC is sometimes also taken to help deal with migraines. Again, we can’t make a medical recommendation for the use of THC for pain, but we can tell you that a lot of people swear by it as a natural alternative to potentially harmful and addictive pain medications.

  1. Antihistamine Benefits

A 2005 study concluded that exposure to THC can suppress mast cell activation, which triggers the release of histamine. Though more research is certainly in order, this could suggest that THC can potentially be a powerful antihistamine, and be a useful product when dealing with allergies. However, be careful not to mix THC with other antihistamine products, because you could experience side effects that might make you dizzy or drowsy.

  1. Nausea

Delta-9 THC has been shown to be effective for settling the stomach, and helping people to keep food down. In fact, the FDA approved a drug made with synthetic THC (called Marinol) to treat nausea caused by chemotherapy and AIDS-related anorexia. Our THC products are not intended for medical use.

  1. Immune Benefits

Researchers have explored the efficacy of delta-9 THC and other cannabinoids as antibacterial agents. THC has been determined to have potent antimicrobial activity against Gram-positive pathogens, like MRSA isolates. Endocannabinoids, the cannabinoids produced by the body and often mimicked by THC and other cannabinoids, have also been shown to be effective in eradicating biofilms.

  1. Antioxidant Benefits

Like CBD and other cannabinoids, THC has been shown to have antioxidant properties. Antioxidants are substances that may protect your cells against free radicals. This may reduce the risk of many diseases.

  1. Muscle Relaxation

THC can help to relax your muscles, which is one of the reasons delta-9 is so effective for stress. Because of the relaxing properties of THC, it can help to get rid of tension in your muscles, which may also help with cramping and related issues.

  1. Sleep 

We’ve now mentioned in several places the relaxation properties of THC, as it works with receptors in the brain. This can make THC a powerful supplement to promote better sleep, both in helping you to fall asleep more easily and to sleep more soundly through the night. 

Full Spectrum CBD + THC Effects

The effects you experience from our Full Spectrum CBD + THC line of products will largely depend on the product itself. In general, if a full spectrum product says that it is sativa based, that means it exhibits the effects of cannabis sativa. These effects are generally mood elevation, creativity, and blissful calmness. Products that are indica based are generally formulated for rest and sleep. When you see a product labeled as hybrid, it means that the product contains characteristics of both cannabis sativa and cannabis indica. In general, these products give you the uplifting mood benefits of a sativa with the relaxation of an indica.

Full Spectrum CBD + THC FAQ

Can you ship delta-9 THC products to my state?

For legal reasons, there are restrictions regarding which CBDfx products can be shipped to certain states. While some products and ingredients may be legal in your state, certain states have restricted products that can be shipped from California. To see if your state is restricted, click here.

What does full spectrum CBD mean?

Full spectrum CBD refers to CBD oil as it exists at the time of extraction, meaning that it contains all of the chemical compounds of the buds, leaves and stalks of the hemp plant.

Can you microdose Delta-9 THC?

Yes! Microdosing delta-9 THC is an excellent way to take advantage of the calming wellness benefits of THC and full spectrum CBD, but without overdoing it. As with all things in the world of delta-9 THC, start small: ¼ the recommended serving size of your THC product in the morning every day — your choice, tincture or gummy. If you want to add to that, take another ¼ serving in the evening. If you feel like that’s still not working, you can bump up the morning serving to ½ serving, and so forth. The key to microdosing THC is to find the serving size that works best for your individual needs!

Does full spectrum CBD make you high?

Full spectrum CBD only contains trace amounts of THC, the cannabinoid that gets you high. Our delta-9 THC products contain additional THC content, though it is still less than the legal limit of 0.3%. That said, THC products may cause psychotropic effects, so we recommend that you adhere to the suggested dosage and avoid driving or operating heavy machinery when you take one of our full spectrum delta-9 products.

What is the difference between THC and delta-9 THC?

Simply said, delta-9 THC is one of a handful of cannabinoids with THC in the name. There are others, but when people generally just say “THC,” they are referring to tetrahydrocannabinol, or delta-9 THC.

How much THC is in Delta-9?

Again, delta-9 is THC.

Is full spectrum CBD better than broad spectrum and isolate?

“Better” is a subjective word. Each of the three types of hemp oil have their advantages. Full spectrum CBD provides a complete blend of all cannabinoids, terpenes, and flavonoids from the hemp plant. So, full spectrum CBD creates the most powerful Entourage Effect. With broad spectrum CBD, the trace amounts of THC have been removed from the oil. This filtration process also takes away a bit of the other chemical compounds. Still, there are plenty of cannabinoids and other compounds to produce a slightly lesser Entourage Effect. The advantage of broad spectrum is that you don’t have to worry about having THC in your system. CBD isolate has all other plant content removed, making it 99% pure CBD. While you don’t achieve an Entourage Effect with CBD isolate, you do have the advantage of removing all the hemp smell and flavor, making it a good choice for some vape and bath products.

Does full spectrum CBD + THC show up in a drug test?

While we use legally compliant CBD oils in all of our products, you can build up THC in your system over time if you regularly use full spectrum products. This can produce a false positive result on some drug tests. Learn more about full spectrum CBD and drug tests here.

Is it legal to buy full spectrum CBD?

Yes, all of our full spectrum products are legally compliant under the 2018 Farm Bill.

Is full spectrum CBD better for anxiety?

Full spectrum CBD produces greater calming effects than other types of CBD oil. It is these effects that people desire in order to reduce stress that can lead to anxiety.

How long does delta-9 THC stay in blood?

The length of time that tetrahydrocannabinol can stay in your system is largely dependent on how much you take. Generally, it can stay in your blood for up to 36 hours. But if you’re concerned about a drug test, you should also know that THC is detectable in your saliva for up to 48 hours, in your urine from three days to a month, and up to 90 days in your hair.

What are the side effects of THC?

THC can cause temporary side effects, including dry mouth, red eyes, increased heart rate, slower reaction time, motor control issues, memory loss, paranoia, and anxiety.

Are delta-8 and delta-10 THC the same thing as delta-9?

No. Delta-9 THC is one of the two major cannabinoids of the cannabis plant (along with CBD). Delta-8 and delta-10 are considered minor cannabinoids, and occur in cannabis in only the tiniest traces.
